Dark Chocolate Coconut Cups

Recipe (makes 6 chocolate cups)
Ingredients
  • 16oz of dark chocolate (70% or higher)
  • 2 Tbsp of coconut oil
  • 1/2 cup of coconut butter (I used this one)
  • Muffin cup liners
Directions

Using a double boiler or microwave, melt the dark chocolate with the coconut oil. While the chocolate and coconut oil do their thing, line a muffin try with your liners (mini ones would be really cute too!)

Once melted, spoon a thin later of chocolate in the bottom of each cup liner (I used 1 1/2 Tbsp for my size of cup). 

Place tray in freezer to set for 10 minutes.

While this layer is freezing, melt your coconut butter over a double boiler. It will not turn really liquidy, (that's definitely not a word) but when you place it on top of your first layer of chocolate, it will lay perfectly, trust me. 

Once your coconut butter is nice and warm, and your first layer of chocolate is firm, spoon a thin layer of the coconut butter into your cups. Then, place the tray back into the freezer to set (10-15 mins).

Once set, spoon your last thin layer of dark chocolate on top of each cup to cover the coconut butter (again 1 1/2 Tbsp or so). Again, allow this layer is set in the freezer for 10-15 minutes.

 

I store my cups in a container in the freezer. You do need to allow them to sit a room temperature for a bit prior to eating them or you may break a tooth! They are the perfect little snack when craving something sweet and chocolatey!

Enjoy!
